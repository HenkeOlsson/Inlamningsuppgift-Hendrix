"Alla vill till himmelen men ingen vill dö" is Timbuktu's fourth studio album. The album was released on February 23, 2005, on the record label JuJu Records. The record sold gold in Sweden and Norway and it features many other famous artists. The first single from the record was "Alla vill till himmelen men ingen vill dö" and it was recorded in Ghana.

The record was ranked by Sonic Magazine in June 2013 as the 26th best album ever in Sweden.

The song "Alla vill till himmelen men ingen vill dö" is taken from the American artist Loretta Lynn's "Everybody wants to go to heaven but nobody wants to die", which was released in 1965 and is to a large amount a translation of the original.

The second and the third single were "Det löser sig", together with Chords and Supreme, which became a big hit in Sweden, and "Stirra ner".

Sagolandet is Timbuktu's seventh studio album and it was released on June 8, 2011. The song "Dansa" which is one of the twelve songs in Sagolandet, was released earlier the same year in March. However the album's first official single was "Dödsdansen" and it was played on the radio in Sweden for the first time in April 2011.

  1. Allsång på gränsen
  2. Dödsdansen

  6. Malmö
Side 2
  1. Ingen tid
  2. Kapitulera
  3. Ingenting enkelt
  4. Livets samba

  6. Dansa

The two following singles after "Dödsdansen" was "Allt grönt" and "Resten av ditt liv". "Resten av ditt liv" was written together with Oskar Linnros, and in 2012 it was nominated a Grammmis for "the song of the year". The song has also sold Platina x3 in Sweden.

The artist himself, Timbuktu, also won a Grammis the same year as "Resten av ditt liv" for "the hiphop/soul of the year" with his record Sagolandet.

Timbuktu with Oskar Linnros, singer/songwriter and producer

Sagolandet was released on the big record label EMI Music, and just like the song "Resten av ditt liv", the record itself has sold Platina in Sweden.
